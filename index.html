Interactive Science Manual

Schwarzschild Radius

Derived by German physicist Karl Schwarzschild, this represents the boundary radius of a spherical, non-rotating mass where escape velocity equals light speed. If an object is squeezed smaller than this radius, it collapses into a black hole.

Event Horizons

Often called the "Point of No Return". Inside the horizon, the warp of spacetime is so radical that any path leading outwards is literally deleted from the laws of physics. Not even photons of light possess enough velocity to escape.

Time Dilation

Einstein's General Relativity shows that gravity warps time. A clock deeper in a gravitational well ticks slower compared to a clock in deep empty space. Right at the Event Horizon, time dilation approaches infinity, appearing to stop.

Escape Velocity

The minimal radial speed required to permanently break away from a gravity source without thrusting. As distance $r$ to the singularity decreases, required escape speed grows. At the event horizon, escape speed reaches the speed of light.

Orbital Motion

Stable circular orbits occur where a body's angular centripetal speed matches gravity's pull. Closer orbits demand extremely fast orbital speeds to avoid plummeting. Inside the Photon Sphere ($r < 1.5 R_s$), circular orbits are impossible.

Photon Rings & Accretion

Photons are bent so heavily near the Event Horizon that they orbit in a perfect sphere at $r=1.5 R_s$. Active Accretion Disks consist of gas spiraling inward, glowing brilliantly from friction and extreme gravitational acceleration.
